Leftovers From Halloween

photogrammetry meets Unity3D and the Oculus

This project was created for the Experimental Capture class at Carnegie Mellon University. Following the theme of "a nostalgic item", I was inspired by my friend's memory of seeing the unwanted Almond Joys leftover at the bottom of the Halloween jar and how she slowly grew to like them as she grew older.

The interactive piece allows the audience, with the help of the Oculus, to see life from the perspective of the battered, neglected candy bar and minimizes the viewer to the same size.

  • Date: November 2015

Two Portraits

from the minds of a synesthetic mathematician and a violinist composer

A collaboration between two other artists, Abby Adams and Tanvi Bajpai, of a different sort, "Two Portraits" seeks to illustrate the unique relationship a musician and a mathematician share with their craft.

The process starts with an original violin composition writen by Abby Adams, whose recording is presented to Tanvi Bajpai, a synesthete. Through listening to music, Tanvi is able "see" numbers associated with different rhythms and notes that is unique to each listening experience (even if the same song). By writing these numbers down, she has essentially translated her musical experience into numbers. I then take these numbers and perform modulo division on each "note" based on the range of a violin, aiming to replicate the original piece's key and rhythm as closely as possible and arranging the different part of the "remix" (bass, main line, vibratto, etc.). The resulting sheet music was then returned to Abby, with the recording above. The original composition can be found here
